# International Relations

## India's bilateral relations

**Q.93 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which country signed a Memorandum of Understanding with India for promoting the production of pigeon pea and other pulses in July 2016?
a) South Africa  b) Syria  c) Egypt  d) Mozambique
**Correct Answer:** d) Mozambique
**Explanation:** To secure long-term pulse supplies, India signed an MoU with **Mozambique** in 2016 to boost the production of **pigeon pea (tur/arhar) and other pulses** there for export to India — part of India's strategy to address its chronic pulse shortfall and price volatility. *(A current-affairs item.)*

**Q.64 (CDS 2018-II)** — The 2+2 Bilateral Dialogue held in September 2018 was between
a) External Affairs and Defence Ministers of India with their US counterparts  b) Finance and Defence Ministers of India with Russia  c) Home and Defence Ministers of India and Pakistan  d) External Affairs and Defence Ministers of India with Pakistan
**Correct Answer:** a) External Affairs and Defence Ministers of India with their US counterparts
**Explanation:** A **"2+2" dialogue** pairs the **foreign and defence ministers** of two countries to coordinate strategic and security matters. The inaugural India-US 2+2 dialogue (September 2018) brought together India's External Affairs and Defence Ministers with the US Secretaries of State and Defense, and produced the COMCASA defence-communications agreement. *(A current-affairs item.)*

**Q.9 (CDS 2025-II)** — The Durand Line is a border demarcation separating which countries?
a) Afghanistan and Iran  b) Afghanistan and Pakistan  c) India and Pakistan  d) Afghanistan and China
**Correct Answer:** b) Afghanistan and Pakistan

**Q.48 (CDS 2022-I)** — Which best describes BRICS?
a) A group of five nuclear powers  b) A group of five industrialized nations  c) A group of five major emerging economies  d) A group of five potential UNSC members from the Global South
**Correct Answer:** c) A group of five major emerging economies
**Explanation:** **BRICS** is best understood as a grouping of five **major emerging (developing) economies** — Brazil, Russia, India, China and South Africa — that cooperate on economic and geopolitical issues and seek a greater voice for the developing world. They are not all nuclear powers or industrialized nations.

**Explanation:** NAM's founding leaders were **Jawaharlal Nehru (India), Gamal Abdel Nasser (Egypt), Josip Broz Tito (Yugoslavia), Sukarno (Indonesia) and Kwame Nkrumah (Ghana)**. **Fidel Castro (Cuba)** rose to prominence in NAM later (he hosted the 1979 summit), but was **not part of the formative-phase core leadership**. Hence d.

**Q.41 (CDS 2024-II)** — About the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO): 1. The official languages are Russian and Chinese. 2. The SCO enjoys observer status in the UN General Assembly. 3. The SCO consists of 13 member states.
a) 1 only  b) 1 and 3  c) 1 and 2  d) 2 and 3
**Correct Answer:** c) 1 and 2
**Explanation:** The SCO's **official working languages are Russian and Chinese** (1), and it holds **observer status at the UN General Assembly** (2). Statement 3 is wrong — the SCO does **not have 13 member states** (after Iran joined in 2023 and Belarus in 2024 it has around 9-10 full members). Hence 1 and 2.

## Regional & sub-regional bodies

**Q.56 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which statement is not correct about the SAARC?
a) Its headquarters is in Kathmandu.  b) China is the only country with Observer status in SAARC.  c) The first SAARC Summit was held in Dhaka.  d) The eighteenth SAARC Summit was held in Nepal.
**Correct Answer:** b) China is the only country with Observer status in SAARC
**Explanation:** SAARC (founded 1985, secretariat at **Kathmandu**) has **multiple observers** — China, the USA, the EU, Japan, Iran, South Korea, etc. — so "China is the only Observer" is false. The first summit was at Dhaka (1985) and the 18th at Kathmandu, Nepal (2014). Hence b is the incorrect statement.

**Q.12 (CDS 2019-II)** — Which statement about the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) is not correct?
a) Its permanent Secretariat is at Jeddah.  b) It works to safeguard the interests of the Muslim world and promote international peace.  c) It is the largest inter-governmental organization in the world.  d) It has consultative and cooperative relations with the UN.
**Correct Answer:** c) It is the largest inter-governmental organization of the world
**Explanation:** The **OIC** (57 member states, secretariat at **Jeddah**) is the **second-largest** inter-governmental organisation **after the United Nations** — so calling it "the largest" (c) is false. The other statements are correct.
