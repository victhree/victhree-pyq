# National Income & Macroeconomics

## GDP, GNP & National Income

**Q.106 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which statement is correct about the composition of national income in India?
a) The share of the manufacturing sector has declined.  b) The share of the services sector has increased sharply.  c) The share of agriculture has remained static.  d) The share of the services sector has declined.
**Correct Answer:** b) The share of services sector has increased sharply
**Explanation:** Over the decades India's economy has shifted toward services, whose **share of GDP has risen sharply** (now well over half). Agriculture's share has steadily fallen (not static), and services have grown, not declined.

**Q.72 (CDS 2017-I)** — About the Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP): 1. Signed by 12 Pacific Rim nations in 2015. 2. Member countries account for about 40% of global GDP. 3. India is a founder member of TPP.
a) 1, 2 and 3  b) 1 and 2 only  c) 2 and 3 only  d) 1 only
**Correct Answer:** b) 1 and 2 only
**Explanation:** The TPP involved **12 Pacific-Rim countries** accounting for roughly **40% of world GDP** (1, 2). **India was never a member** of the TPP, so statement 3 is false.

**Q.75 (CDS 2017-II)** — The base year of India's new GDP series was shifted from 2004-05 to
a) 2007-08  b) 2008-09  c) 2010-11  d) 2011-12
**Correct Answer:** d) 2011-12
**Explanation:** India revised the GDP base year to **2011-12** (in the 2015 revision), also moving to GVA-based measurement at market prices.

**Q.112 (CDS 2018-II)** — Per updated World Bank data for 2017, India is the sixth biggest economy by GDP. Which one is not ahead of India?
a) Japan  b) UK  c) France  d) Germany
**Correct Answer:** c) France
**Explanation:** India overtook **France** to become the world's sixth-largest economy; Japan, the UK and Germany were still ahead at that time. *(A current-affairs item.)*

**Q.43 (CDS 2019-II)** — Indexation is a method associated with which of the following?
a) Controlling inflation  b) Nominal GDP estimation  c) Measurement of savings rate  d) Fixing of wage compensation
**Correct Answer:** d) Fixing of wage compensation
**Explanation:** **Indexation links payments (wages, pensions, dearness allowance) to a price index** so they automatically rise with inflation, protecting real incomes.

**Q.1 (CDS 2021-I)** — Which statement is not correct?
a) Real GDP is calculated by valuing outputs of different years at common prices.  b) Potential GDP is the real GDP the economy would produce with fully employed resources.  c) Nominal GDP is calculated by valuing outputs of different years at constant prices.  d) Real GDP per capita is real GDP divided by population.
**Correct Answer:** c)
**Explanation:** **Nominal GDP uses current (year's own) prices**, not constant prices — so statement c is false. Real GDP is the one that uses constant/base-year prices.

**Q.26 (CDS 2021-I)** — A situation where equilibrium real GDP falls short of potential GDP is known as
a) Recessionary gap  b) Inflationary gap  c) Demand-side inflation  d) Supply-side inflation
**Correct Answer:** a) Recessionary gap
**Explanation:** When actual output is **below potential (full-employment) output**, the shortfall is a **recessionary (deflationary) gap**, indicating under-utilised resources.

**Q.49 (CDS 2021-II)** — Which statement about social-sector spending in India during 2014-19 (States + Union together) is true?

**Q.67 (CDS 2023-I)** — Which indicator is included in the National Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)?

**Q.66 (CDS 2024-I)** — 1. The GDP deflator captures the average price of an unchanging basket of commodities. 2. The GDP deflator can measure real GDP but not the inflation rate.

**Correct Answer:** d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Explanation:** The **GDP deflator covers all goods/services in current output (a changing basket)** — the "fixed basket" is the CPI — so statement 1 is wrong. And the deflator **can** measure inflation (it is itself a price index), so statement 2 is wrong too.

**Q.73 (CDS 2024-I)** — Per capita GDP depends upon: 1. Proportion of population in the working age. 2. Work participation rate. 3. Per-worker productivity.
a) 3 only  b) 1 and 3 only  c) 1 and 2 only  d) 1, 2 and 3
**Correct Answer:** d) 1, 2 and 3
**Explanation:** Per capita GDP = (working-age share) × (participation rate) × (output per worker), so it depends on **all three** factors.

**Q.75 (CDS 2024-I)** — Which statement is not correct for National Income Accounting in India?

**Correct Answer:** b)
**Explanation:** **Net factor income from abroad is added to GDP to get GNP** — it is not part of GDP itself — so statement b is false. The other three are correct accounting conventions.

**Q.76 (CDS 2025-II)** — 1. Effective capital expenditure as % of GDP increased from 2020-21 to 2023-24. 2. Expenditure on revenue account as % of GDP increased from 2020-21 to 2023-24.

**Q.118 (CDS 2025-II)** — Which is a good statistic to evaluate where an economy stands in the financial cycle?
a) Tax/GDP Ratio  b) Fiscal Deficit/GDP Ratio  c) Household Consumption/GDP Ratio  d) Credit/GDP Ratio
**Correct Answer:** d) Credit/GDP Ratio
**Explanation:** The **credit-to-GDP ratio (and its "gap")** is the standard indicator of the financial cycle — rising credit signals expansion/build-up of risk, falling credit signals contraction.

## Theories & Indicators of Growth

**Q.91 (CDS 2016-II)** — According to the Classical Theory of Employment, deviations from full employment are
a) purely temporary in nature  b) permanent in nature  c) imaginary situations  d) normal situations
**Correct Answer:** a) purely temporary in nature
**Explanation:** Classical economists held that the economy automatically tends to **full employment**, so any unemployment is only a **temporary** disequilibrium corrected by flexible wages/prices.

**Q.92 (CDS 2016-II)** — Capital deepening refers to
a) going for more fixed capital per worker  b) emphasis on social overhead capital  c) constant capital-output ratio  d) increasing capital-output ratio
**Correct Answer:** a) going for more fixed capital per worker
**Explanation:** **Capital deepening** means raising the amount of **capital per worker** (more machines/tools per worker), boosting labour productivity.

**Explanation:** The Twelfth Plan identified **poverty, group inequality, regional imbalance and unemployment** as the key challenges to inclusive growth — all four.

**Q.28 (CDS 2017-II)** — The phenomenon of demographic dividend relates to

**Q.34 (CDS 2020-II)** — Normally, there will not be a shift in the demand curve when
a) price of the commodity falls  b) consumers want to buy more at any given price  c) average income rises  d) population grows
**Correct Answer:** a) price of a commodity falls
**Explanation:** A change in the **commodity's own price causes movement along** the demand curve, not a shift. Shifts come from changes in income, population or tastes.

**Q.35 (CDS 2020-II)** — A market with a large number of firms, a homogeneous product, infinite elasticity of demand for an individual firm and no price control is

**Explanation:** These are the textbook features of **perfect competition** — many firms, identical product, price-taking firms facing perfectly elastic demand.

# Inflation, Money & Prices

## WPI, CPI & Inflation

**Q.100 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which index is now used by the Reserve Bank of India to measure the rate of inflation?
a) NASDAQ Index  b) BSE Index  c) Consumer Price Index  d) Wholesale Price Index
**Correct Answer:** c) Consumer Price Index
**Explanation:** Since the move to inflation targeting (2016), the RBI uses **CPI (Combined) headline inflation** as its key measure (target 4% ± 2%).

**Q.69 (CDS 2021-II)** — Match: A. Lorenz curve  B. Phillips curve  C. Engel curve  D. Laffer curve | 1. Inflation and employment  2. Tax rates and tax revenue  3. Inequality in income/wealth distribution  4. Income and proportion of expenditure on food.
a) A-3 B-4 C-1 D-2  b) A-2 B-1 C-4 D-3  c) A-3 B-1 C-4 D-2  d) A-2 B-4 C-1 D-3
**Correct Answer:** c) A-3 B-1 C-4 D-2
**Explanation:** Lorenz → income/wealth inequality (3); Phillips → inflation vs unemployment (1); Engel → income vs share spent on food (4); Laffer → tax rate vs tax revenue (2).

**Q.9 (CDS 2022-II)** — Which is not one of the Eight Core Industries?
a) Coal  b) Refinery products  c) Rubber products  d) Cement
**Correct Answer:** c) Rubber products
**Explanation:** The Eight Core Industries are coal, crude oil, natural gas, refinery products, fertilizers, steel, cement and electricity — **rubber products is not among them**.

**Q.20 (CDS 2023-I)** — Which situation can lead to inflation?
a) Rapid growth of aggregate demand outweighing supply  b) Sluggish growth of aggregate demand  c) Reduction in money supply  d) Higher levels of unemployment
**Correct Answer:** a) Rapid growth of aggregate demand outweighing supply
**Explanation:** **Demand-pull inflation** occurs when aggregate demand grows faster than supply, pulling prices up. The other options are disinflationary.

**Q.65 (CDS 2023-I)** — Which measure can the Government use to combat inflation?
a) Increasing non-plan expenditure on defence, police, etc.  b) Providing more export subsidies  c) Increasing the rate of interest on savings and fixed deposits  d) Reducing the cash reserve ratio (CRR)
**Correct Answer:** c) Increasing the rate of interest on savings and fixed deposits
**Explanation:** **Higher interest rates encourage saving and reduce spending**, curbing demand-pull inflation. The other options would expand demand/money supply and worsen inflation.

**Q.32 (CDS 2023-II)** — Effects of devaluation/depreciation of currency: 1. Increase in imports and decrease in exports. 2. Increase in exports and decrease in imports. 3. Increase in domestic inflation. 4. Decrease in domestic inflation.
a) 1 and 3 only  b) 1 and 4 only  c) 2 and 3 only  d) 3 only
**Correct Answer:** c) 2 and 3 only
**Explanation:** A weaker currency makes **exports cheaper and imports dearer (so exports rise, imports fall — 2)** and raises the price of imported goods, **fuelling domestic inflation (3)**.

**Q.65 (CDS 2023-II)** — Wholesale Price inflation rose in India during 2021-22 due to: 1. Sharp rise in international crude oil prices. 2. Decrease in economic activity post-Covid. 3. Disruption of global supply chains. 4. High freight cost.
a) 2 and 3 only  b) 1, 3 and 4  c) 1 and 2 only  d) 1 and 4 only
**Correct Answer:** b) 1, 3 and 4
**Explanation:** **Rising crude prices (1), supply-chain disruption (3) and high freight costs (4)** drove WPI inflation; economic activity was **recovering**, not falling, so statement 2 is wrong.

**Q.31 (CDS 2024-I)** — Regarding the weightage of articles in the Wholesale Price Index (WPI): 1. Fuel and power have higher weightage in WPI primary articles. 2. The weightage of manufactured products in WPI is higher than that of fuel and power.

**Q.63 (CDS 2025-I)** — While calculating the Consumer Price Index, housing as an item is classified as

**Correct Answer:** d) Core service

**Q.67 (CDS 2025-I)** — Core inflation is

## Foreign Exchange & Currency

**Q.107 (CDS 2016-II)** — In India, the term "hot money" refers to
a) Currency Reserves with the RBI  b) Net GDR  c) Net Foreign Direct Investment  d) Foreign Portfolio Investment
**Correct Answer:** d) Foreign Portfolio Investment

a) prices of domestic goods remain constant  b) exports become cheaper to importers  c) imports remain constant  d) exports rise proportionately

**Q.66 (CDS 2023-I)** — The sustained decrease in the general price level is called
a) deflation  b) stagflation  c) devaluation  d) recession
**Correct Answer:** a) deflation

**Q.30 (CDS 2023-II)** — Which government actions would contract the money supply? 1. Purchase of Treasury Bills by the central bank from the public. 2. Sale of Treasury Bills by the central bank to the public. 3. Sale of foreign exchange by the central bank. 4. Purchase of foreign exchange by the central bank.
a) 1 and 4 only  b) 1 and 3 only  c) 2 and 3 only  d) 2 only

**Explanation:** **Selling T-bills (2)** and **selling foreign exchange (3)** both **absorb rupees from the system**, contracting money supply. Buying T-bills or forex (1, 4) injects money.

**Q.95 (CDS 2024-II)** — Which is not included in the Capital Account of the Balance of Payments?
a) Foreign Direct Investment  b) Commercial Borrowing  c) Invisibles  d) External Assistance
**Correct Answer:** c) Invisibles
**Explanation:** **"Invisibles" (services, transfers, income) are part of the Current Account**, not the Capital Account. FDI, commercial borrowings and external assistance are capital-account items.

**Q.66 (CDS 2025-I)** — How can currency depreciation stimulate an increase in net exports? 1. By reducing export costs. 2. By reducing import prices.
a) 1 only  b) 2 only  c) Both 1 and 2  d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Correct Answer:** a) 1 only
**Explanation:** Depreciation makes a country's **exports cheaper for foreign buyers (1)**, boosting them. It **raises (not reduces) import prices**, so statement 2 is wrong.
