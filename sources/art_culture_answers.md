# Performing Arts

## Classical Dance

**Q.33 (CDS 2025-I)** — Which one among the following is the earliest text?
a) Natyashastra of Bharata  b) Sushruta Samhita  c) Ashtadhyayi  d) Manusmriti
**Correct Answer:** c) Ashtadhyayi
**Explanation:** Panini's **Ashtadhyayi**, the great treatise on Sanskrit grammar, is the earliest of these — dated to roughly the **5th–4th century BCE**. The **Natyashastra** (Bharata's text on drama, dance and music) and the **Manusmriti** were compiled around 200 BCE–200 CE, and the **Sushruta Samhita** (surgery/medicine) reached its known form somewhat later. So Ashtadhyayi predates the others.

## Folk & Tribal Dance

**Q.44 (CDS 2017-II)** — Teejan Bai, a recipient of the M. S. Subbulakshmi Centenary Award 2016, is an exponent of
a) Kannada classical vocal  b) Kajari dance  c) Bihu dance  d) Pandavani, a traditional performing art
**Correct Answer:** d) Pandavani, a traditional performing art
**Explanation:** **Teejan Bai** is the legendary performer of **Pandavani** — a folk ballad-singing tradition of Chhattisgarh in which tales from the Mahabharata are narrated through powerful singing and dramatic gesture, the performer holding a tambura. A Padma Vibhushan awardee, she popularised this art worldwide.

## Hindustani & Carnatic Music

**Q.82 (CDS 2018-I)** — What is the name of the literary genre developed by the Khojas, a branch of the Ismaili sect?
a) Ginan  b) Ziyarat  c) Raag  d) Shahada
**Correct Answer:** a) Ginan
**Explanation:** **Ginans** are devotional hymns/poems of the **Nizari Ismaili (Khoja) community**, composed in Indian languages (Gujarati, Sindhi, Hindi). Sung in melodious form, they convey mystical and ethical teachings and blend Islamic and Indic traditions. (Ziyarat = pilgrimage/visitation; Shahada = the Islamic declaration of faith; Raag is a melodic framework, not a literary genre.)

**Q.111 (CDS 2024-I)** — Renowned classical singer Prabha Atre, who passed away recently, was an exponent of which Gharana?
a) Kirana Gharana  b) Patiala Gharana  c) Gwalior Gharana  d) Agra Gharana
**Correct Answer:** a) Kirana Gharana
**Explanation:** A **gharana** is a stylistic "school" or lineage of Hindustani classical music. **Prabha Atre** (Padma Vibhushan), one of the most celebrated vocalists of her time, belonged to the **Kirana Gharana** — a school known for its emphasis on the precise intonation of individual notes (swara) and slow, elaborate melodic development. *(A current-affairs/obituary item.)*

## Painting Styles & Schools

**Q.59 (CDS 2020-I)** — The famous Mughal painting depicting Jahangir embracing the Safavid king Shah Abbas was painted by
a) Abd al-Samad  b) Abul Hasan  c) Dasavant  d) Bishandas
**Correct Answer:** b) Abul Hasan
**Explanation:** This allegorical painting — Jahangir dreaming of embracing his rival Shah Abbas of Persia, the two standing on a lion and a lamb over a globe — was painted by **Abul Hasan**, whom Jahangir honoured with the title **"Nadir-uz-Zaman" (Wonder of the Age)**. **Q.49 (CDS 2024-I)** — Which has been an important early Chola painting discovered at the Brihadisvara Temple, Thanjavur in 1931?
a) Buddha with Chauri-bearers on either side  b) Shiva as Yoga-Dakshinamurti  c) Yakshi Kali  d) Parshvanatha with snake-hood on a lion-throne
**Correct Answer:** b) Shiva as Yoga-Dakshinamurti  *(verify)*
**Explanation:** The **Brihadisvara (Rajarajeshvara) Temple at Thanjavur**, built by Rajaraja Chola I, preserves early **Chola murals** that were discovered beneath later Nayaka-period paintings in 1931. Being a great Shaiva temple, its paintings depict Shiva themes — including **Shiva as Dakshinamurti** and Nataraja. (The Buddha-with-chauri-bearers theme belongs to Ajanta, and the Parshvanatha/Yakshi options are Jain.) *(Worth confirming against the official key.)*

## Painters & Artists

**Q.34 (CDS 2020-I)** — Who was one of the founders of the Indian Society of Oriental Art?

## Buddhist / Jain / Hindu architecture

**Q.50 (CDS 2022-I)** — About sculptures at Sanchi: 1. Some sculptures represent Shalabhanjika. 2. The Shalabhanjika figure is a woman surrounded by lotuses and elephants. 3. The Shalabhanjika motif was not directly inspired by Buddhist ideas.
a) 1, 2 and 3  b) 2 and 3 only  c) 2 only  d) 1 and 3 only
**Correct Answer:** d) 1 and 3 only
**Explanation:** The great Buddhist stupa at **Sanchi** carries, on its toranas (gateways), the **Shalabhanjika** — a graceful woman shown grasping the branch of a tree (statement 1). This is an ancient **folk fertility/auspiciousness motif, not a Buddhist invention** — it was absorbed into Buddhist art (statement 3). Statement 2 is wrong: a woman **surrounded by lotuses and elephants** describes **Gaja-Lakshmi**, not the Shalabhanjika. Hence 1 and 3 only.

**Correct Answer:** b) 2 only
**Explanation:** The **State Emblem of India** is indeed adapted from the **Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath** (the four-lion capital), with the motto "Satyameva Jayate" added below (statement 2 correct). **Correct Answer:** d) Neither 1 nor 2

## Harappan & Ancient sites

**Q.115 (CDS 2016-II)** — About Harappan cities: 1. Roads were not always absolutely straight and did not always cross one another at right angles. 2. A striking feature is the uniformity in the average size of the bricks for houses and city walls.
a) 1 only  b) 2 only  c) Both 1 and 2  d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Correct Answer:** c) Both 1 and 2
**Explanation:** Harappan town-planning is famous for its grid layout, but research shows roads were **not perfectly straight nor always at exact right angles** (statement 1), and a hallmark of the civilisation was the **standardised brick size** (ratio 1:2:4) used uniformly for houses and city walls alike (statement 2). Both statements are correct.

**Q.94 (CDS 2017-II)** — Which statement about the Harappan Culture is not correct?
a) It witnessed the first cities in the subcontinent.  b) It marks the first use of script, written from right to left.  c) It marks the earliest known use of iron as a medium for sculpting.  d) It marks the earliest known use of stone as a medium for sculpting.
**Correct Answer:** c) It marks the earliest known use of iron as a medium for the art of sculpting
**Explanation:** The Harappan (Indus Valley) Civilisation produced the subcontinent's **first cities (a)**, an as-yet-undeciphered **script written right-to-left (b)**, and early **stone sculpture** (e.g. the "Priest-King," d). But the Harappans belonged to the **Bronze Age and did not use iron at all** — iron came centuries later — so statement c is false.

## Inscriptions, Coins & Ashokan Pillars

**Q.114 (CDS 2018-I)** — Who deciphered the Brahmi and Kharoshthi scripts?
a) Piyadassi  b) Colin Mackenzie  c) Alexander Cunningham  d) James Prinsep
