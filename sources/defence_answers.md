# Indian Army

## Army Commands & Theatre

**Q.99 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which statement is not correct about the Andaman and Nicobar Command?
a) It is the first integrated theatre command in India.  b) Its headquarters is at Port Blair.  c) It is commanded by a three-star officer.  d) It was set up by the British during the Second World War.
**Correct Answer:** d) It was set up by the British during the Second World War
**Explanation:** The **Andaman and Nicobar Command (ANC)** is India's **first (and so far only) tri-service integrated theatre command**, set up in **2001** with its headquarters at **Port Blair**, headed in rotation by a three-star officer from the three services. Statement d is false — it is a post-independence creation (2001), **not** a British wartime command.

**Q.103 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which is not one of the Commands of the Indian Army?
a) South Western Command  b) North Eastern Command  c) Central Command  d) Army Training Command (ARTRAC)
**Correct Answer:** b) North Eastern Command
**Explanation:** The Indian Army has **seven commands** — six operational (Northern, Eastern, Western, South Western, Southern, Central) and one training (**ARTRAC**, at Shimla). There is **no "North Eastern Command"** — the north-east falls under the **Eastern Command** (Kolkata). Hence b.

## Regiments & Corps

**Q.101 (CDS 2016-II)** — Jammu and Kashmir Rifles is
a) an infantry regiment of the Indian Army  b) a battalion of the Rashtriya Rifles  c) the Armed Police of the State of J&K  d) a paramilitary force under the Ministry of Home Affairs
**Correct Answer:** a) an infantry regiment of the Indian Army
**Explanation:** The **Jammu and Kashmir Rifles** is a regular **infantry regiment of the Indian Army** (with origins in the erstwhile princely state forces of J&K). It should not be confused with the **Rashtriya Rifles** (a counter-insurgency force) or the state armed police.

**Q.102 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which one of the following is correct?
a) The Infantry Regiment is also known as the Mechanized Infantry Regiment.  b) The Corps of Electrical and Mechanical Engineers is subsumed in the Corps of Engineers.  c) The Army Medical Corps and Army Dental Corps are divisions of the Army Service Corps.  d) The Army has its own Corps of Air Defence.
**Correct Answer:** d) Army has its own Corps of Air Defence
**Explanation:** The **Army Air Defence (AAD)** is a separate **arm/corps of the Indian Army**, responsible for protecting field forces and vital installations from air attack (guns and SAMs). The other statements are wrong: infantry and mechanized infantry are distinct; the **Corps of EME** is separate from the Corps of Engineers; and the Medical/Dental Corps are independent of the Army Service Corps.

## Ranks & Hierarchy

**Q.100 (CDS 2016-II)** — Who is the Chairman of the Chiefs of Staff Committee?
a) The Chief of Army Staff  b) The Chief of Naval Staff  c) The Chief of Air Staff  d) The member who has been the longest on the Committee
**Correct Answer:** d) The member who has been the longest on the Committee
**Explanation:** Under the system in force when this question was set (before the **Chief of Defence Staff** was created in 2019), the **Chiefs of Staff Committee** was chaired on rotation by the **senior-most service chief — the one with the longest tenure on the committee**. (Since 2019, the CDS is the permanent chairman.)

**Q.104 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which statement is correct?
a) The rank of Second Lieutenant has been abolished.  b) The Chief of Army Staff carries the rank of Field Marshal.  c) All officers posted to Andaman and Nicobar Islands wear the ranks of the Indian Navy.  d) The rank of Major General is higher than Lieutenant General.
**Correct Answer:** a) The rank of Second Lieutenant has been abolished
**Explanation:** The Army's entry-level commissioned rank of **Second Lieutenant was abolished (around 2004–05)** — officers are now commissioned directly as Lieutenant. The others are wrong: the COAS is a **General** (Field Marshal is an honorary five-star rank held only twice); and **Lieutenant General is higher than Major General** (so d is reversed).

**Q.104 (CDS 2017-I)** — The rank of Captain in the Indian Navy is equivalent to
a) Captain of the Indian Army  b) Group Captain in the Indian Air Force  c) Lieutenant Colonel of the Indian Army  d) Wing Commander of the Indian Air Force
**Correct Answer:** b) Group Captain in the Indian Air Force
**Explanation:** Across the three services, a Navy **Captain** equates to an Army **Colonel** and an Air Force **Group Captain** (b). (An Army **Captain** is several ranks junior — equivalent to a Navy Lieutenant — which is the trap in option a.)

**Q.84 (CDS 2018-I)** — Which is not a correct ascending order of commissioned ranks in the Indian defence forces?
a) Lieutenant, Captain, Major, Lt Colonel, Colonel, Brigadier, Major General, Lt General, General  b) Flying Officer, Flight Lieutenant, Squadron Leader, Commander, Group Captain, Air Commodore, Air Vice Marshal, Air Marshal, Air Chief Marshal  c) Flying Officer, Flight Lieutenant, Squadron Leader, Group Captain, Wing Commander, Air Commodore, Air Vice Marshal, Air Marshal, Air Chief Marshal  d) Sub Lieutenant, Lieutenant, Lieutenant Commander, Commander, Captain, Commodore, Rear Admiral, Vice Admiral, Admiral
**Correct Answer:** c) (the Air Force sequence with Group Captain placed before Wing Commander)  *(see note — option b is also flawed)*
**Explanation:** The correct IAF order is Flying Officer → Flight Lieutenant → Squadron Leader → **Wing Commander → Group Captain** → Air Commodore → Air Vice Marshal → Air Marshal → Air Chief Marshal. Option c is wrong because it **places Group Captain before Wing Commander** (an inversion). Note that option b is *also* defective — it inserts "Commander" (a naval rank) and omits Wing Commander — so the item appears to have two incorrect options; the intended keyed answer is the c-inversion. *(Flagged for cross-checking.)*

**Q.65 (CDS 2018-II)** — Who is the Chairman of the Defence Planning Committee set up in April 2018?
a) The Prime Minister  b) The National Security Advisor  c) The Defence Minister  d) The Chief of the Army Staff
**Correct Answer:** b) The National Security Advisor
**Explanation:** The **Defence Planning Committee (DPC)**, created in April 2018 as a permanent body to give defence and national-security planning long-term coherence, is **chaired by the National Security Advisor (NSA)**; its members include the three service chiefs and the defence, foreign and expenditure secretaries.

## Army equipment (Tanks, Artillery, APCs)

**Q.54 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which does not signify a battle tank?
a) T-55  b) T-155  c) T-72  d) T-90
**Correct Answer:** b) T-155
**Explanation:** The **T-55, T-72 and T-90** are all main battle tanks of the Soviet/Russian "T-series" (the T-72 and T-90 are mainstays of the Indian Army). **"T-155"** is **not a tank** — it refers to a 155 mm self-propelled howitzer (the Turkish "Firtina"). Hence b.

**Q.73 (CDS 2016-II)** — The Government cleared production of 18 indigenous Dhanush artillery guns to be produced by
a) Indian Army  b) US Army  c) Indian Ordnance Factory Board  d) Indian and US Army jointly
**Correct Answer:** c) Indian Ordnance Factory Board
**Explanation:** The **Dhanush** is an indigenous 155 mm towed howitzer — an upgraded, "Indianised" version of the Bofors gun — produced by the **Ordnance Factory Board (OFB)**, earning it the nickname "desi Bofors."

**Q.66 (CDS 2018-II)** — Tejas is the name of which of the following?
a) Main battle tank  b) Nuclear submarine  c) Light combat aircraft  d) Aircraft carrier
**Correct Answer:** c) Light combat aircraft
**Explanation:** **Tejas** is India's indigenous **Light Combat Aircraft (LCA)**, designed by the ADA and built by HAL — a single-engine, multirole fighter inducted into the Indian Air Force.

**Q.89 (CDS 2018-II)** — Which manufacturer is engaged in upgrading the Swedish 155-mm Bofors Howitzer under Project Dhanush?
a) Bharat Electronics Limited  b) Ordnance Factory Board  c) Bharat Dynamics Limited  d) Mishra Dhatu Nigam
**Correct Answer:** b) Ordnance Factory Board
**Explanation:** The **Ordnance Factory Board** produces the **Dhanush** gun, derived from the licence-produced Bofors design. (BEL makes electronics; BDL makes missiles; MIDHANI makes special alloys/metals.)

**Q.15 (CDS 2019-I)** — Prahaar is a
a) battle tank  b) surface-to-surface missile  c) aircraft carrier  d) submarine
**Correct Answer:** b) surface-to-surface missile
**Explanation:** **Prahaar** is an indigenous, road-mobile, short-range **surface-to-surface tactical ballistic missile** developed by DRDO (range ~150 km), meant to fill the gap between unguided rockets (Pinaka) and longer-range missiles (Prithvi).

**Q.81 (CDS 2021-I)** — Which is correct about Pinaka?
a) It is a multi-barrel rocket system.  b) It is a type of battle tank.  c) It is an advanced submarine.  d) It is an indigenously developed drone system.
**Correct Answer:** a) It is a multi-barrel rocket system
**Explanation:** **Pinaka** is an indigenous **Multi-Barrel Rocket Launcher (MBRL)** system developed by DRDO — a battery can fire a salvo of rockets to saturate a target area within seconds. It is named after Lord Shiva's bow.

**Q.105 (CDS 2021-II)** — Pinaka, developed in India, is a
a) battle tank  b) multi-barrel rocket launcher  c) anti-tank guided missile  d) armoured utility vehicle
**Correct Answer:** b) multi-barrel rocket launcher
**Explanation:** As above, **Pinaka is a multi-barrel rocket launcher** system — a key area-bombardment artillery weapon of the Indian Army, with progressively longer-range guided variants now in service.

# Indian Navy

## Naval Commands & Headquarters

**Q.80 (CDS 2017-I)** — The headquarters of the Southern Naval Command is located at
a) Karwar  b) Kochi  c) Thiruvananthapuram  d) Chennai
**Correct Answer:** b) Kochi

## Naval Ships, Submarines & Carriers

**Q.106 (CDS 2017-I)** — The fast attack craft INS Tihayu (commissioned October 2016) is based at
a) Kochi  b) Panaji  c) Visakhapatnam  d) Mumbai
**Correct Answer:** c) Visakhapatnam
**Explanation:** **INS Tihayu**, a Water Jet Fast Attack Craft built by GRSE, was based at **Visakhapatnam** under the Eastern Naval Command, for coastal patrol and low-intensity maritime operations. *(A current-affairs item.)*

**Q.36 (CDS 2018-I)** — Which is not an exclusive right of a coastal nation over its Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ)?
a) Survey and exploitation of mineral/ocean-deposit resources  b) Exploitation of marine energy and organisms including fishing  c) Conservation and management of marine resources  d) Navigation of ships and laying down submarine cables
**Correct Answer:** d) Navigation of ships and laying down submarine cables
**Explanation:** Under UNCLOS, a coastal state has **sovereign rights over the resources** of its EEZ (out to 200 nautical miles) — exploiting minerals, marine energy, fishing, and conserving resources (a, b, c). But **freedom of navigation and the laying of submarine cables/pipelines are rights enjoyed by all states** in the EEZ, not exclusive to the coastal nation. Hence d. *(A maritime-law item.)*

**Q.88 (CDS 2018-II)** — What is India's first Indigenous Aircraft Carrier (IAC) called?
a) Vikrant  b) Virat  c) Vaibhav  d) Varaha
**Correct Answer:** a) Vikrant
**Explanation:** **INS Vikrant (IAC-1)** is India's first indigenously designed and built aircraft carrier, constructed at Cochin Shipyard and commissioned in **2022** — making India one of a handful of nations able to build its own carriers. (The earlier INS Vikrant of 1961 was British-built.)

**Q.14 (CDS 2019-II)** — Which IN ships participated in SIMBEX-19? 1. INS Kolkata 2. INS Shakti 3. INS Vikrant.
a) 1, 2 and 3  b) 1 and 2 only  c) 2 and 3 only  d) 1 only
**Correct Answer:** b) 1 and 2 only
**Explanation:** **SIMBEX** is the annual **Singapore-India Maritime Bilateral Exercise**. In the 2019 edition, the Indian Navy was represented by the destroyer **INS Kolkata** and the fleet tanker **INS Shakti** (1, 2). **INS Vikrant was not yet commissioned** in 2019, so statement 3 is wrong. Hence 1 and 2 only.

**Q.116 (CDS 2020-II)** — Which is a decommissioned aircraft carrier?

**Correct Answer:** d) INS Viraat
**Explanation:** **INS Viraat** (formerly HMS Hermes), once the world's oldest serving carrier, was **decommissioned in 2017**. (INS Rajput was a destroyer, INS Chakra a leased nuclear submarine, and INS Khanderi a Scorpene-class submarine.)

**Q.118 (CDS 2020-II)** — The Andaman & Nicobar islands were connected to the mainland by a Submarine Optical Fibre Cable. Which island was not connected initially?
a) Shaheed Island  b) Swaraj Island  c) Little Andaman  d) Port Blair

**Q.88 (CDS 2021-I)** — Which ship was involved in Mission Sagar II?
a) INS Shakti  b) INS Vikramaditya  c) INS Kesari  d) INS Airavat
**Correct Answer:** c) INS Kesari
**Explanation:** **Mission Sagar** was India's COVID-era humanitarian outreach to friendly Indian Ocean nations, delivering food, medicines and medical teams. **INS Kesari**, a landing ship, carried out both Mission Sagar I and **Mission Sagar II** (to South Sudan, Sudan, Djibouti, Eritrea), reflecting India's "SAGAR" (Security and Growth for All in the Region) vision. *(A current-affairs item.)*

**Q.113 (CDS 2021-II)** — INS Tabar participated in a two-day naval exercise with the Italian Navy. It was conducted in the

**Q.100 (CDS 2022-II)** — The P-75 I (P-75 India) project refers to the manufacture of
a) Diesel-electric submarines  b) Nuclear-powered submarines  c) Aircraft carrier warships  d) Field guns
**Correct Answer:** a) Diesel-electric submarines
**Explanation:** **Project-75I** is the Indian Navy's programme to build six advanced **conventional (diesel-electric) submarines** with **Air-Independent Propulsion (AIP)** — a follow-on to Project-75 (the Scorpene/Kalvari-class), under the "Make in India" defence push.

**Q.119 (CDS 2024-II)** — Which statement about INS Tarmugli is not correct?

**Q.106 (CDS 2025-II)** — Which statement about Nilgiri, Surat and Vagsheer is correct?

All three statements are correct. *(A current-affairs item.)*

## Indian Coast Guard

**Q.72 (CDS 2018-I)** — Which statements about the role of the Indian Coast Guard are correct? 1. It has been entrusted with offshore security coordination authority. 2. It is the lead intelligence agency for the coastal and sea border. 3. Coastal security in territorial waters.
a) 1 and 3 only  b) 1, 2 and 3  c) 1 and 2 only  d) 3 only
**Correct Answer:** a) 1 and 3 only

## Aircraft & Squadrons

**Q.62 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which statement is not correct?
a) BrahMos is a supersonic cruise missile made by India and Russia.  b) The name BrahMos comes from the Brahmaputra and Moskva rivers.  c) Indian scientists integrated BrahMos with the Sukhoi-30 MKI fighter.  d) DRDO is the manufacturer of the Sukhoi-30 MKI fighter.
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** The **Sukhoi-30 MKI is manufactured under licence by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL)**, not DRDO — so statement d is false. **Q.87 (CDS 2016-II)** — The first Tejas squadron of the IAF consists of
a) 20 aircraft with 4 in reserve  b) 100 aircraft with 10 in reserve  c) 20 aircraft with no reserve  d) 25 aircraft with 5 in reserve
**Correct Answer:** a) 20 aircraft with 4 in reserve
**Explanation:** A fighter squadron in the IAF is typically **16 aircraft plus 4 reserves = 20**. The first Tejas squadron — **No. 45 Squadron, the "Flying Daggers"** — was raised on this establishment. Hence a.

**Q.74 (CDS 2017-I)** — HAL handed over the first two indigenously designed and manufactured light combat aircraft to the IAF in 2016. They are named
a) Marut  b) Garud  c) Tejas  d) Pushpak
**Correct Answer:** c) Tejas
**Explanation:** The indigenous Light Combat Aircraft is the **HAL Tejas**, the first two production examples of which were handed over to the IAF in 2016 (forming the Flying Daggers squadron). (The HF-24 Marut was India's earlier indigenous fighter of the 1960s.)

**Q.116 (CDS 2025-II)** — About India's military arsenal: 1. HAMMER is a weapon system built for the Rafale fighter aircraft. 2. The SCALP cruise missile is also known as Storm Shadow. 3. BrahMos missiles are built under a joint venture between India and Russia.
a) 1 and 2 only  b) 2 and 3 only  c) 1, 2 and 3  d) 3 only
**Correct Answer:** c) 1, 2 and 3
**Explanation:** All three are correct: **HAMMER** is a French precision-guided air-to-ground munition integrated on the **Rafale** (1); the air-launched cruise missile **SCALP is the French name for the same weapon the British call Storm Shadow** (2); and **BrahMos is the India-Russia joint venture** (3). Hence all three.

# Missiles & Defence Technology

## Missiles (Agni, Prithvi, BrahMos, Akash)

**Q.105 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which is not an Air Defence Missile system?
a) Akash  b) Trishul  c) Tatra  d) Astra
**Correct Answer:** c) Tatra
**Explanation:** **Tatra** is a heavy **military truck/vehicle** (used to carry equipment and launchers), not a missile. **Akash** (medium-range surface-to-air) and **Trishul** (short-range surface-to-air) are air-defence missiles, and **Astra** is an air-to-air missile. Hence c.

**Q.73 (CDS 2018-I)** — Which is India's first indigenously designed and developed long-range subsonic cruise missile, deployable from multiple platforms?
a) Astra  b) Akash  c) Nirbhay  d) Shankhnaad
**Correct Answer:** c) Nirbhay
**Explanation:** **Nirbhay** is India's first indigenous **long-range subsonic cruise missile** (range ~1,000 km), capable of terrain-hugging flight and launch from land, sea and air platforms — comparable to the US Tomahawk. (Astra is an air-to-air missile; Akash is a SAM.)

**Q.107 (CDS 2021-II)** — The range capability of the Agni-P ballistic missile is
a) 1,000 - 2,000 km  b) 2,000 - 3,000 km  c) 3,000 - 4,000 km  d) 4,000 - 5,000 km
**Correct Answer:** a) 1,000 - 2,000 km
**Explanation:** **Agni-P (Agni-Prime)** is a new-generation, canisterised, two-stage **medium-range ballistic missile** with a range of about **1,000–2,000 km** — lighter and more advanced than the earlier Agni-I and Agni-II it is meant to replace.

**Q.102 (CDS 2022-II)** — About the BrahMos missile: 1. It has a flight range of up to 290 km. 2. It can attain a speed of 2.8 Mach. 3. Its cruising altitude can be up to 30 km.
a) 1 and 2  b) 2 only  c) 1 and 3  d) 3 only
**Correct Answer:** a) 1 and 2
**Explanation:** The original **BrahMos** had a range of about **290 km** (1) and flies at about **Mach 2.8** — nearly three times the speed of sound (2), making it one of the fastest cruise missiles in the world. Its cruising altitude is up to **about 15 km**, not 30 km — so statement 3 is wrong. Hence 1 and 2.

**Q.112 (CDS 2023-II)** — From which country has India procured the S-400 Triumf air-defence missile system?
a) United Kingdom  b) U.S.A.  c) Russia  d) France
**Correct Answer:** c) Russia
**Explanation:** India contracted to buy the **S-400 Triumf** — a long-range, mobile **surface-to-air missile (air-defence) system** capable of engaging aircraft, drones and missiles at up to 400 km — from **Russia**, despite the risk of US sanctions (CAATSA).

## Strategic systems & MTCR

**Q.86 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which statement about the Missile Technology Control Regime is not correct?
a) It is an informal, voluntary partnership initially formed among the G-7 countries.  b) Members prevent proliferation of missile/UAV technology capable of carrying above 500 kg payload more than 300 km.  c) China applied to join but members did not offer membership.  d) No other country outside the membership follows the Regime rules.
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** The **MTCR** (1987) is an informal, voluntary export-control regime (founded by the G-7) to curb the spread of missiles/UAVs able to deliver a **≥500 kg payload over 300 km** (a, b correct), and China's bid was indeed not accepted (c). Statement d is false — some **non-member states voluntarily adhere** to MTCR guidelines. *(A current-affairs/strategic item.)*

**Q.54 (CDS 2017-I)** — India became a member of which grouping in 2016?
a) Non-Proliferation Treaty  b) Missile Technology Control Regime  c) Nuclear Suppliers Group  d) Wassenaar Arrangement
**Correct Answer:** b) Missile Technology Control Regime
**Explanation:** India joined the **MTCR in 2016** as a full member — a step that eased access to advanced missile/UAV technology.
