# Physics

## Mechanics

**Q.20 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which one of the following physical quantities has the same dimensions as that of Planck's constant (h)?
a) Linear momentum  b) Angular momentum  c) Force  d) Potential energy
**Correct Answer:** b) Angular momentum
**Explanation:** Planck's constant has dimensions of energy × time = J·s = kg·m²·s⁻¹, which is the dimension of **action** and also of **angular momentum** (L = mvr). Linear momentum is kg·m·s⁻¹, force is kg·m·s⁻², and energy is kg·m²·s⁻² — all dimensionally different from h.

**Q.21 (CDS 2016-II)** — Which one of the following statements is not correct?
a) The response of the ear to sound of increasing intensity is approximately logarithmic.  b) The sensitivity of the human ear does not vary with the frequency of the sound.  c) When two or more waves traverse the same medium, the displacement of any element is the algebraic sum of the displacements due to each wave.  d) Longitudinal waves can travel in all media — solids, liquids and gases.
**Correct Answer:** b)
**Explanation:** Ear sensitivity is strongly **frequency-dependent** — humans hear best around 2–5 kHz and poorly near 20 Hz or 20 kHz — so statement b is false. The logarithmic response (basis of the decibel scale), the principle of superposition, and the ability of longitudinal waves to travel through all material media are all correct.

**Q.38 (CDS 2016-II)** — The energy of a photon whose momentum is 10 MeV/c, where c is the speed of light, is
a) 10 MeV  b) 100 MeV  c) 1 MeV  d) 0.1 MeV
**Correct Answer:** a) 10 MeV
**Explanation:** For a photon (massless), energy and momentum are related by **E = pc**. With p = 10 MeV/c, E = (10 MeV/c)·c = **10 MeV**. The other values would require a different momentum–energy relation, which does not apply to light.

**Q.12 (CDS 2017-I)** — For which one of the following does the centre of mass lie outside the body?
a) A fountain pen  b) A cricket ball  c) A ring  d) A book
**Correct Answer:** c) A ring
**Explanation:** The centre of mass of a **ring** lies at its geometric centre — which is empty space, i.e. **outside the material of the body**. For a solid pen, ball or book the centre of mass falls within the material itself.

**Q.45 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which statement regarding the jet stream is not correct?
a) It is a narrow band of high-velocity wind.  b) It follows a wavy path near the tropopause at elevations of 8–15 km.  c) Jet streams are typically continuous over long distances.  d) In summer, the polar front jet achieves its maximum force.
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** The **polar front jet stream is strongest in winter**, when the temperature contrast between polar and tropical air is greatest; in summer it weakens and shifts poleward — so statement d is false. Jet streams are indeed narrow, fast, wavy bands of wind near the tropopause. *(This is essentially a geography question.)*

**Q.76 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which statements are correct? 1. In June 2016 the LIGO group announced detection of a second set of gravitational waves. 2. The waves were generated by the merger of two black holes about 1.4 billion light-years distant. 3. The waves were generated by collision of two white dwarf stars about 1.4 billion light-years distant.
a) 1 only  b) 1 and 2 only  c) 2 and 3 only  d) 1, 2 and 3
**Correct Answer:** b) 1 and 2 only
**Explanation:** LIGO's second confirmed detection (GW151226) was announced in **June 2016** (1) and came from a **black-hole merger** (2). Gravitational waves come from merging compact objects (black holes/neutron stars), **not** white-dwarf collisions, so statement 3 is false.

**Q.11 (CDS 2017-II)** — An electron and a proton starting from rest are accelerated through a potential difference of 1000 V. Which statement is correct?
a) The kinetic energy of both particles will be different.  b) The speed of the electron will be higher than that of the proton.  c) The speed of the proton will be higher than that of the electron.  d) The speeds will be equal.
**Correct Answer:** b)
**Explanation:** Both gain the **same kinetic energy**, KE = qV, since the magnitude of their charge is equal. But KE = ½mv², and the electron is ~1836 times lighter than the proton, so for equal KE the **electron acquires a much higher speed**.

**Q.59 (CDS 2017-II)** — An object on a horizontal frictionless surface has gravity and the normal reaction acting on it, giving zero net force. What can be said about its motion (after being given a push)?
a) It moves with acceleration.  b) It moves with deceleration.  c) It moves with constant speed but varying direction.  d) It moves with constant velocity.
**Correct Answer:** d) The object will move with constant velocity.
**Explanation:** Gravity (down) and the normal force (up) cancel, so the **net force is zero**. By Newton's first law a body with no net force moves in a **straight line at constant velocity** (uniform speed and unchanging direction). *(The question's reference to the third law is a misstatement — the balancing of gravity and normal force is not a third-law action-reaction pair.)*

**Q.10 (CDS 2018-I)** — Consider: 1. There is no net moment on a body in equilibrium. 2. The momentum of a body is always conserved. 3. The kinetic energy of an object is always conserved. Which are correct?
a) 1, 2 and 3  b) 2 and 3 only  c) 1 and 2 only  d) 1 only
**Correct Answer:** d) 1 only
**Explanation:** In equilibrium the net torque (moment) is zero (1, correct). Momentum is conserved **only when no external force acts**, and kinetic energy is conserved only in elastic interactions — so the blanket claims in 2 and 3 are false. Hence only statement 1 holds.

**Q.16 (CDS 2018-I)** — Statement I: Tides are caused by the combined gravitational forces of the Moon and Sun and the Earth's rotation. Statement II: The Earth rotates from west to east.
a) Both true, II correctly explains I  b) Both true, II not the correct explanation of I  c) I true, II false  d) I false, II true
**Correct Answer:** b)
**Explanation:** Both statements are individually true, but the Earth's **west-to-east rotation** is not what *causes* tides — tides arise mainly from the **Moon's (and Sun's) gravitational pull**. So II is true but does not explain I.

**Q.40 (CDS 2018-I)** — The pressure of a fluid varies with depth as P = P₀ + ρgh, where ρ is the fluid density. This expression is associated with
a) Pascal's law  b) Newton's law  c) Bernoulli's principle  d) Archimedes' principle
**Correct Answer:** a) Pascal's law
**Explanation:** P = P₀ + ρgh is the **hydrostatic pressure** relation, which follows from **Pascal's law** — pressure in a static fluid increases linearly with depth and is transmitted equally in all directions. Bernoulli applies to moving fluids; Archimedes deals with buoyancy.

**Q.7 (CDS 2018-II)** — A particle moves from rest with uniform acceleration. The percentage increase in displacement during the sixth second compared to the fifth second is about
a) 11  b) 22  c) 33  d) 44
**Correct Answer:** b) 22
**Explanation:** Distance in the nth second (from rest) = a(2n−1)/2. Fifth second = 4.5a, sixth second = 5.5a. Increase = (5.5 − 4.5)/4.5 ≈ **22%**.

**Q.9 (CDS 2018-II)** — The position vector of a particle is r = 7î + 2t²ĵ + 3t k̂. Then the instantaneous velocity and acceleration respectively lie
a) on xy-plane and along z-direction  b) on yz-plane and along x-direction  c) on yz-plane and along y-direction  d) on xy-plane and along x-direction
**Correct Answer:** c) on yz-plane and along y-direction
**Explanation:** Velocity v = dr/dt = 4t ĵ + 3 k̂ — it has only y and z components, so it lies in the **yz-plane**. Acceleration a = dv/dt = 4 ĵ — purely along the **y-direction**. *(Reconstructed from the garbled source vector.)*

**Q.83 (CDS 2019-I)** — For an ideal gas, which statement does not hold true?
a) The speed of all gas molecules is the same.  b) The kinetic energies of all gas molecules are not the same.  c) The potential energy of the gas molecules is zero.  d) There is no interactive force between the molecules.
**Correct Answer:** a)
**Explanation:** Gas molecules follow the **Maxwell–Boltzmann speed distribution** — they have a wide range of speeds, not a single common speed, so statement a is false. In an ideal gas there are no intermolecular forces (hence zero potential energy), and molecular kinetic energies do vary.

**Q.17 (CDS 2019-II)** — The Sasakawa Award of the United Nations is given for work in the field of
a) Disaster Reduction  b) Peace Keeping  c) Health Services  d) Poverty Alleviation
**Correct Answer:** a) Disaster Reduction
**Explanation:** The **UN Sasakawa Award** is the most prestigious international award recognising excellence in **disaster risk reduction**. *(A current-affairs/awards item rather than physics.)*

**Q.44 (CDS 2019-II)** — A car undergoes uniform circular motion. Its acceleration is
a) zero  b) a non-zero constant  c) non-zero but not a constant  d) None of the above
**Correct Answer:** c) a non-zero but not a constant
**Explanation:** In uniform circular motion the speed is constant but the **direction of velocity changes continuously**, producing a centripetal acceleration. Its magnitude is constant but its direction keeps changing, so as a **vector it is not constant** (and not zero).

**Q.86 (CDS 2020-II)** — If the linear momentum of a moving object is doubled by applying a force, its kinetic energy will
a) remain the same  b) increase four times  c) increase two times  d) increase eight times
**Correct Answer:** b) increase by four times
**Explanation:** Kinetic energy relates to momentum as **KE = p²/2m**. Doubling p multiplies p² by 4, so the kinetic energy becomes **four times** its original value.

**Q.42 (CDS 2021-I)** — When rivers discharge their waters from all directions into a lake or depression, the pattern is known as
a) Trellis  b) Dendritic  c) Radial  d) Centripetal
**Correct Answer:** d) Centripetal
**Explanation:** In a **centripetal** drainage pattern, streams converge **inward** from all sides into a central basin, lake or depression. (Radial is the opposite — streams flowing outward from a dome; dendritic is tree-like; trellis is rectangular.) *(A geography item.)*

**Q.118 (CDS 2021-I)** — The direction of acceleration in uniform circular motion is along the
a) direction of motion  b) tangent to the circle at the point of observation  c) direction of velocity  d) direction perpendicular to velocity
**Correct Answer:** d) direction perpendicular to velocity
**Explanation:** The acceleration is **centripetal**, pointing toward the centre of the circle, which is always **perpendicular to the (tangential) velocity**. It is not along the direction of motion or the tangent.

**Q.22 (CDS 2021-II)** — A bus starting from rest with uniform acceleration attains 20 km/h in 10 minutes. Its acceleration is
a) 200 km/h²  b) 120 km/h²  c) 100 km/h²  d) 240 km/h²
**Correct Answer:** b) 120 km/h²
**Explanation:** Acceleration = change in velocity ÷ time = 20 km/h ÷ (10/60 h) = 20 ÷ 0.1667 = **120 km/h²**.

**Q.21 (CDS 2022-I)** — An object weighs 9 N on Earth. Its weight on a planet where g is 9 times that of Earth would be
a) remain the same  b) 1 N  c) become 9 times  d) reduced to 1/9 N
**Correct Answer:** c) The weight would become 9 times
**Explanation:** Weight = mg. The mass is unchanged, but if **g becomes 9 times** larger, the weight becomes 9 × 9 = 81 N — i.e. **nine times** the Earth value.

**Q.22 (CDS 2022-I)** — The twinkling of a star is due to
a) atmospheric reflection of starlight  b) atmospheric refraction of starlight  c) continuous change in the position of the star  d) oscillation of starlight
**Correct Answer:** b) atmospheric refraction of starlight
**Explanation:** Twinkling (stellar scintillation) occurs because starlight passes through atmospheric layers of **varying density and refractive index**, which continuously bend (refract) the light, making the star's apparent brightness and position fluctuate.

**Q.102 (CDS 2022-I)** — Match vaccine with type: 1. Covaxin – Inactivated pathogen-based  2. Covishield – mRNA vaccine  3. Sputnik V – Viral vector-based. Which pairs are correctly matched?
a) 1 only  b) 2 and 3 only  c) 1 and 3 only  d) 1, 2 and 3
**Correct Answer:** c) 1 and 3 only
**Explanation:** Covaxin is an **inactivated-virus** vaccine (1, correct) and Sputnik V is a **viral-vector** vaccine (3, correct). Covishield is **not** an mRNA vaccine — it is a **viral-vector (adenovirus)** vaccine — so pair 2 is wrong. (The mRNA vaccines were Pfizer and Moderna.)

**Q.24 (CDS 2022-II)** — An object of mass 10 kg moves at uniform speed 10 m/s. Its linear momentum and kinetic energy respectively are
a) 100 N·s and 500 J  b) 100 N·s and 1000 J  c) 200 N·s and 500 J  d) 200 N·s and 1000 J
**Correct Answer:** a) 100 N·s and 500 J
**Explanation:** Momentum p = mv = 10 × 10 = **100 N·s**; kinetic energy = ½mv² = ½ × 10 × 100 = **500 J**.

**Q.83 (CDS 2022-II)** — An object is dropped from a height onto the floor. Which quantity remains uniform as it falls?
a) Its acceleration  b) Its momentum  c) Its kinetic energy  d) Its potential energy
**Correct Answer:** a) Its acceleration
**Explanation:** During free fall only gravity acts, so the **acceleration stays constant at g** (≈9.8 m/s²). Speed increases, so momentum and kinetic energy rise, while potential energy falls — only the acceleration is uniform.

**Q.87 (CDS 2022-II)** — The acceleration due to gravity at the Earth's surface depends on
a) its mass only  b) its radius only  c) both its mass and radius  d) either its mass or its radius
**Correct Answer:** c) both its mass and radius
**Explanation:** Surface gravity is g = **GM/R²**, so it depends on **both** the planet's mass M (directly) and its radius R (inversely as the square).

**Q.9 (CDS 2023-I)** — The area under the velocity–time graph for a particle moving in a straight line with uniform acceleration gives
a) its average velocity  b) its net displacement  c) the distance travelled  d) its average speed
**Correct Answer:** b) its net displacement
**Explanation:** The **area under a velocity–time graph equals displacement** (velocity × time). For motion in one direction this also equals distance, but in general the signed area gives **net displacement**.

**Q.36 (CDS 2023-II)** — A charged particle moves through a magnetic field B with velocity v. The force F is
a) maximum when v and B are parallel  b) maximum when v and B are anti-parallel  c) maximum when v and B are perpendicular  d) independent of the angle between v and B
**Correct Answer:** c) maximum when v and B are perpendicular to each other
**Explanation:** The magnetic force is F = qvB sinθ. It is **maximum when θ = 90°** (v ⟂ B, sinθ = 1) and **zero** when v is parallel or anti-parallel to B (sinθ = 0).

**Q.74 (CDS 2023-II)** — About the Race to Zero Campaign: 1. A main objective is to build momentum around the shift to a decarbonized economy. 2. It is related to the theory of Zero-Sum Game.
a) 1 only  b) 2 only  c) Both 1 and 2  d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Correct Answer:** a) 1 only
**Explanation:** The **Race to Zero** is a global UN-backed campaign to mobilise net-zero/**decarbonization** commitments (1, correct). It has nothing to do with the game-theory "zero-sum game" concept, so statement 2 is wrong.

**Q.14 (CDS 2024-I)** — Which correctly describes the working principle of an atomic clock?
a) Vibration of a small quartz crystal  b) Simple harmonic motion of atoms inside a crystal  c) Resonant frequency in cesium or rubidium atom  d) Excitation and de-excitation of hydrogen atoms
**Correct Answer:** c) Resonant frequency in cesium or rubidium atom
**Explanation:** Atomic clocks keep time using the extremely stable **resonant (hyperfine transition) frequency of atoms such as caesium or rubidium** — the SI second is defined by 9,192,631,770 cycles of a caesium-133 transition. Quartz vibration is used in ordinary watches, not atomic clocks.

**Q.47 (CDS 2024-I)** — Identify the drainage pattern: most common; uniform rock type and regular slope; map looks like veins on a leaf with smaller streams joining the main stream at an acute angle.
a) Dendritic  b) Radial  c) Trellis  d) Centripetal
**Correct Answer:** a) Dendritic
**Explanation:** A **dendritic** pattern is the commonest, developing on uniform rock and gentle regular slopes; its tree/leaf-vein branching with tributaries meeting at **acute angles** is its signature. *(A geography item.)*

**Q.90 (CDS 2024-I)** — 1. Angular velocity is the same for all locations on Earth's surface while linear velocity varies. 2. Linear velocity is maximum at the equator and minimum at the poles.
a) 1 only  b) 2 only  c) Both 1 and 2  d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Correct Answer:** c) Both 1 and 2
**Explanation:** Every point on Earth completes one rotation in 24 hours, so **angular velocity is identical everywhere** (1, correct). Linear (tangential) speed = ωr depends on distance from the axis, which is largest at the **equator** and zero at the **poles** (2, correct).

**Q.23 (CDS 2025-I)** — A motorcycle has initial velocity 5 m/s; after 3 s its velocity is 7 m/s. Its displacement in 3 s is
a) 21 m  b) 18 m  c) 36 m  d) 6 m
**Correct Answer:** b) 18 m
**Explanation:** For uniform acceleration, displacement = average velocity × time = [(5 + 7)/2] × 3 = 6 × 3 = **18 m**.

**Q.25 (CDS 2025-I)** — Which statement is not correct?
a) All bodies attract each other due to gravitational force.  b) Newton's law of gravitation follows from Newton's laws of motion.  c) The value of g at the centre of the Earth is zero.  d) Mass and weight are measured in different units.
**Correct Answer:** b)
**Explanation:** **Newton's law of gravitation is an independent law**, not derived from his three laws of motion — so statement b is false. Universal attraction, g = 0 at Earth's centre, and mass (kg) versus weight (newton) being different units are all correct.

**Q.23 (CDS 2025-II)** — The linear momentum of a particle is conserved if
a) the net force on it is maximum  b) the net force on it is zero  c) the net torque on it is zero  d) the net work done on it is maximum
**Correct Answer:** b) the net force on it is zero
**Explanation:** From Newton's second law, F = dp/dt, so momentum stays constant **only when the net external force is zero**. Torque governs angular momentum, and work relates to kinetic energy — neither conserves linear momentum.

**Q.24 (CDS 2025-II)** — When a stone tied to a string is whirled in a circle, the work done on it by the string is
a) positive  b) negative  c) zero  d) depends on the mass of the stone
**Correct Answer:** c) is zero
**Explanation:** The string's tension provides the centripetal force, which points toward the centre — **perpendicular to the stone's motion**. Since work = F·d·cos90° = 0, the string does **no work** on the stone.

**Q.40 (CDS 2025-II)** — The reaction K₂SO₄(aq) + BaCl₂(aq) → 2KCl(aq) + BaSO₄(s) is an example of
a) Addition reaction  b) Displacement reaction  c) Decomposition reaction  d) Double displacement reaction
**Correct Answer:** d) Double displacement reaction
**Explanation:** Two compounds **exchange their ions** (K⁺ pairs with Cl⁻, Ba²⁺ with SO₄²⁻), and an insoluble precipitate (BaSO₄) forms — the defining feature of a **double displacement (precipitation) reaction**. *(Chemistry item placed in Mechanics by the source.)*

## Heat & Thermodynamics

**Q.15 (CDS 2017-I)** — Which is the most characteristic property of an element?
a) Density  b) Boiling point  c) Mass number  d) Atomic number
**Correct Answer:** d) Atomic number
**Explanation:** An element's identity is fixed by its **atomic number** (number of protons) — this never changes for a given element. Density and boiling point vary with conditions, and the mass number differs between isotopes of the same element.

**Q.97 (CDS 2017-II)** — Tuvalu has been in discussion recently. Why?
a) A potato plant that grows at high altitude  b) A place in equatorial Africa where snow is found  c) New technology to meet global warming  d) A country under threat of submergence due to ice melting and sea-level rise
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** **Tuvalu**, a low-lying Pacific island nation, is among the countries most threatened by **rising sea levels** caused by climate change, risking submergence. *(A climate/current-affairs item.)*

**Q.7 (CDS 2018-I)** — The correct relation between Kelvin temperature T and Celsius temperature t is
a) two independent scales  b) T = t  c) T = t − 273.15  d) T = t + 273.15
**Correct Answer:** d) T = t + 273.15
**Explanation:** The Kelvin and Celsius scales have the **same size of degree** but a shifted zero — 0 °C = 273.15 K — so **T = t + 273.15**.

**Q.32 (CDS 2018-I)** — Which represents the relation "heat lost = heat gained"?
a) Principle of thermal equilibrium  b) Principle of colours  c) Principle of calorimetry  d) Principle of vaporization
**Correct Answer:** c) Principle of calorimetry
**Explanation:** The **principle of calorimetry** states that, in an isolated system, the **heat lost by the hot body equals the heat gained by the cold body** until thermal equilibrium is reached. This is the basis of all heat-exchange (mixing) calculations.

**Q.13 (CDS 2018-II)** — Which is not a primary function of a green leaf?
a) Manufacture of food  b) Interchange of gases  c) Evaporation of water  d) Conduction of food and water
**Correct Answer:** d) Conduction of food and water
**Explanation:** Leaves carry out **photosynthesis (food-making), gas exchange and transpiration (water evaporation)**. **Conduction/transport** of food and water is the job of the **vascular tissues (xylem and phloem in the stem)**, not a primary leaf function. *(A biology item.)*

**Q.32 (CDS 2018-II)** — In which case is heat loss primarily not due to convection?
a) Boiling water  b) Land and sea breeze  c) Circulation of air around a blast furnace  d) Heating of the glass surface of a bulb due to current in the filament
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** A bulb's glass is heated mainly by **radiation (and conduction)** from the glowing filament, not by convection. Boiling water, land/sea breezes and air circulation around a furnace are all classic **convection** processes.

**Q.95 (CDS 2019-II)** — About mixtures: 1. A substance can be separated into other kinds of matter by any physical process. 2. Dissolved sodium chloride can be separated from water by the physical process of evaporation.
a) 1 only  b) 2 only  c) Both 1 and 2  d) Neither 1 nor 2
**Correct Answer:** b) 2 only
**Explanation:** A **pure substance cannot** be split into other kinds of matter by physical means (only mixtures can), so statement 1 is wrong. Dissolved salt **can** be recovered from water by **evaporation**, a physical process (2, correct).

**Q.24 (CDS 2020-I)** — If x is the temperature in Kelvin and y in °C, the correct relation is
a) x = 273 − y  b) x = 273 + y  c) x = 173 + y  d) x = 173 − y
**Correct Answer:** b) x = 273 + y
**Explanation:** Kelvin = Celsius + 273 (using the rounded value), so **x = 273 + y**.

**Q.43 (CDS 2023-I)** — Xylem conducts water upward. Which xylem tissue consists of living cells?

**Correct Answer:** c) Xylem parenchyma
**Explanation:** Among xylem components, only **xylem parenchyma is living**; tracheids, vessels and fibres are **dead, lignified** cells at maturity. Parenchyma stores food and helps in short-distance transport. *(A biology item.)*

**Q.103 (CDS 2023-I)** — Identify the cloud: born through convection; only type producing hail, thunder and lightning; large cauliflower-shaped towers, often with anvil tops.

**Q.15 (CDS 2024-II)** — A new concrete surface is covered with wet straw/gunny bags. This is done to
a) prevent fast evaporation until hydration has proceeded well  b) protect from dust  c) prevent fungus  d) give a smoother, cleaner surface
**Correct Answer:** a)
**Explanation:** This is **curing** of concrete. Keeping the surface moist **slows water loss by evaporation** so that the cement's **hydration reactions** can proceed fully, giving the concrete its strength.

**Q.22 (CDS 2024-II)** — Which heat transfer is an example of convection?
a) Heating food in a microwave oven  b) Boiling water in a pot on a gas stove  c) Feeling warmth in the sun  d) Heating a brass rod at one end and feeling the rise at the other
**Correct Answer:** b) Boiling water in a pot on a gas stove
**Explanation:** In boiling water, heated water rises and cooler water sinks, setting up **convection currents** — the bulk movement of fluid carrying heat. Microwave heating and sunlight are **radiation**, and heat moving along a brass rod is **conduction**.

## Light & Optics

**Q.40 (CDS 2016-II)** — Light travels in a straight line (rectilinear propagation). This holds if the medium is
a) of variable refractive index  b) made up of slabs of different refractive indices  c) homogeneous and transparent  d) inhomogeneous and transparent
**Correct Answer:** c) homogeneous and transparent
**Explanation:** Light travels straight only when the medium has a **uniform (homogeneous) refractive index**; any change in refractive index bends the ray. A transparent, homogeneous medium offers no such variation, so light goes straight.

**Q.30 (CDS 2017-I)** — An X-ray photon has 1 keV energy, a visible photon 3 eV. Which statement is not correct?
a) The X-ray wavelength is less than the visible wavelength.  b) The two photons have different energies.  c) The speeds of both photons in vacuum are different.  d) The X-ray frequency is higher than the visible frequency.
**Correct Answer:** c)
**Explanation:** **All electromagnetic radiation travels at the same speed c in vacuum**, regardless of energy or frequency — so statement c is false. The higher-energy X-ray correctly has a shorter wavelength and higher frequency.

**Q.31 (CDS 2017-I)** — The optical phenomenon primarily responsible for a rainbow is
a) diffraction  b) interference  c) dispersion  d) reflection
**Correct Answer:** c) dispersion
**Explanation:** A rainbow forms when sunlight entering raindrops is **dispersed** — different colours refract by different amounts (and undergo internal reflection), splitting white light into the spectrum.

**Q.13 (CDS 2017-II)** — A ray strikes a plane mirror at 40° to the surface normal. On reflection it is deviated by
a) 40°  b) 100°  c) 90°  d) 80°
**Correct Answer:** b) 100°
**Explanation:** The deviation produced by a plane mirror is **180° − 2i**, where i is the angle of incidence. With i = 40°, deviation = 180° − 80° = **100°**.

**Q.14 (CDS 2017-II)** — Regarding infrared, visible and ultraviolet radiation, which statement is not correct?
a) IR wavelength is more than UV wavelength.  b) UV wavelength is smaller than visible.  c) Photon energy of visible light is more than IR.  d) Photon energy of UV is lesser than that of visible light.
**Correct Answer:** d)
**Explanation:** Energy rises as wavelength shortens (E = hc/λ). **UV has a shorter wavelength than visible light, so its photon energy is greater, not lesser** — statement d is false. The other comparisons are correct.

**Q.20 (CDS 2017-II)** — Which type of glass is used for making optical instruments?
a) Pyrex glass  b) Soft glass  c) Hard glass  d) Flint glass
**Correct Answer:** d) Flint glass
**Explanation:** **Flint glass**, with its high refractive index and dispersion, is used for lenses and prisms in optical instruments. Pyrex is heat-resistant borosilicate glassware; soft/hard glasses are for general use.

**Q.32 (CDS 2017-II)** — Statement I: Skin cancer is generally caused by ultraviolet radiation. Statement II: The stratosphere allows ultraviolet radiation to enter the Earth from the Sun.
a) Both true, II explains I  b) Both true, II not the explanation  c) I true, II false  d) I false, II true
**Correct Answer:** c) Statement I is true but Statement II is false
